The Legacy

More than a basketball player

"The most important thing is to try and inspire people so that they can be great in whatever they want to do."
- Kobe Bryant

Global Impact

How Kobe influenced basketball and culture worldwide

International Ambassador

Kobe helped grow basketball's popularity globally, especially in China and Europe, where he became one of the most beloved athletes.

Inspiring Generations

His work ethic and dedication inspired countless young players who grew up watching him, including many current NBA stars.

Mamba Mentality

Kobe's philosophy of constant improvement and relentless pursuit of excellence transcended sports and influenced people in all walks of life.

Cultural Icon

Beyond basketball, Kobe became a cultural icon whose influence extended to fashion, business, and entertainment.

Tributes & Memorials

How the world honored Kobe's memory

Staples Center Memorial

Thousands gathered at the Staples Center, now Crypto.com Arena, to pay their respects following Kobe's passing.

February 24, 2020

NBA All-Star Game Format Change

The NBA changed the All-Star Game format to honor Kobe, with the final target score being 24 points more than the leading team's score after three quarters.

February 16, 2020

Murals Worldwide

Artists around the world created murals honoring Kobe and Gianna, with Los Angeles becoming a canvas for dozens of tributes.

2020-Present

Lakers Jersey Retirement

The Lakers retired both of Kobe's jersey numbers, 8 and 24, making him the only player in team history with two retired numbers.

December 18, 2017

Beyond Basketball

Kobe's impact extended far beyond the court

Philanthropy & Giving Back

Kobe was dedicated to making a positive impact through various charitable initiatives:

  • Co-founded the Kobe and Vanessa Bryant Family Foundation to help youth in need and reduce homelessness in Los Angeles
  • Granted more than 200 wishes through the Make-A-Wish Foundation
  • Supported After-School All-Stars, providing after-school programs to children across the United States
  • Created the Mamba Sports Foundation (now Mamba & Mambacita Sports Foundation) to provide underserved communities with sports programs

Business Ventures & Investments

Kobe built an impressive business portfolio after his basketball career:

  • Co-founded Bryant Stibel, a venture capital firm that invested in technology, media, and data companies
  • Invested $6 million in sports drink BodyArmor, which later became worth over $200 million
  • Founded Granity Studios, a multimedia content company focused on creating new ways to tell stories around sports
  • Launched the Mamba Sports Academy, a training facility for young athletes

Storytelling & Creative Works

Kobe channeled his creativity into various storytelling projects:

  • Created "Dear Basketball," an animated short film based on his retirement letter that won an Academy Award
  • Authored "The Mamba Mentality: How I Play," a book detailing his approach to the game
  • Created "The Wizenard Series," a young adult fantasy sports series
  • Produced "Detail," an ESPN+ series analyzing basketball gameplay and strategy

Family Life

Beyond his public persona, Kobe was a devoted family man:

  • Married Vanessa Laine Bryant in 2001, and they remained together for 19 years until his passing
  • Father to four daughters: Natalia, Gianna, Bianka, and Capri
  • Coached Gianna's basketball team and was passionate about promoting women's basketball
  • Known for commuting by helicopter to spend more time with his family

Mamba & Mambacita Sports Foundation

Continuing Kobe and Gianna's legacy through sports

The Mamba & Mambacita Sports Foundation is a nonprofit organization dedicated to creating positive impact for underserved athletes and boys and girls in sports.

The Foundation was created through the vision and loving memory of Kobe and Gianna "Gigi" Bryant.

Mission

To provide opportunities to underserved athletes and young women in sports.
